HERBIVORE

Elephant

Elephas maximus / Loxodonta africana

Asian elephants are native to South and Southeast Asia, such as India, Sri Lanka, and Indonesia. African elephants are found in various parts of Africa, mainly in savannas and tropical forests.

Cow

Bos taurus

Domestic cattle originated in Southwest Asia, around what is now Turkey, Iran, and Mesopotamia (modern Iraq).

Goat

Capra aegagrus hircus

Domestic goats originated in the Western and Central Asian region, more precisely from the mountainous areas that now include countries like Iran, Turkey, and Afghanistan.

Hippo

Hippopotamus amphibius

Hippos are large animals native to Sub-Saharan Africa. They live in bodies of water such as rivers, lakes, or swamps that are deep enough for them to wade and submerge their bodies.

Rabbit

Oryctolagus cuniculus

Domestic rabbits originated in Europe and North Africa, more precisely from an area that now includes Spain, Portugal, and the northern part of Africa. The wild ancestor of the domestic rabbit is the European rabbit, which lives in grasslands, forests, and rocky areas.

Kangaroo

Macropus rufus

Giraffe

Giraffa camelopardalis

Giraffes are large mammals native to the Sub-Saharan African region. Giraffes live in open grasslands and sparse forests throughout southern, eastern, and western Africa.

Camel

Camelus dromedarius

Camels are large mammals native to the Middle East and North Africa region. They have long been used by humans as transportation and load-carrying animals in the vast, harsh deserts.

Zebra

Equus quagga

Zebras are large mammals native to the Sub-Saharan African region. Zebras live in grasslands and savannas, as well as open areas in many parts of Africa, especially in areas rich with grass and water sources.
