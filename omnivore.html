Skunk

Mephitis mephitis

Skunks are small mammals known for their ability to emit a foul odor from glands under their tails as a defense mechanism. They are mostly found in North America, especially in open areas, grasslands, forests, and rural areas.

Monkey

Primates

Large monkeys are usually found in tropical forests and rainforests in Africa and Asia, while small monkeys are more common in tropical and subtropical regions, including the rainforests of South America, Asia, and Africa.

Hedgehog

Erinaceus europaeus

A small mammal is known for its protective layer of spines. They are usually found in habitats such as forests, grasslands, and bushes in areas of Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

Turtle

Testudo / Chelonia mydas

Land tortoises, such as Testudo prefer to live in deserts, grasslands, and dry forests, while freshwater tortoises, such as live in rivers, lakes, and swamps. Meanwhile, marine turtles, such as green turtles, live in tropical and subtropical ocean waters, often seen on coral reefs or beaches.

Raven

Corvus corax

Ravens are all-consuming birds known for their high intelligence and exceptional adaptability. Crows can be found in a variety of habitats, including forests, mountains, grasslands, as well as urban areas, from the northern hemisphere to colder regions.

Ostrich

Struthio camelus

The world's largest flightless bird, but very fast at running. It is native to the deserts and open areas of Africa and the Middle East, including areas such as the Sahara, and much of the sub-Saharan region, as well as parts of the Arabian Peninsula.

Bear

Ursidae

They are found in a variety of habitats, depending on the species, including forests, mountains, tundra, as well as polar regions. Brown and black bears commonly live in forests, grasslands, and mountains in North America, Europe, and Asia, while polar bears can be found in cold Arctic regions, such as around the North Pole.

Wild Boar

Sus scrofa

They are widespread in Europe, Asia, and parts of Africa, and have been introduced to other regions, including North America and Australia. Wild boars prefer to live in dense forests and heavily vegetated areas to find food and shelter from predators.

Dog

Canis lupus familiaris

In the beginning, dogs lived in the wild, mainly in forests, grasslands, and open areas, hunting in packs for food. However, after the process of domestication, dogs have become an integral part of human life and are found in almost every corner of the world.
